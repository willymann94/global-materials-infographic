D. Cobalt: The Battery Metal

Global Production (2023)

The cobalt market is defined by extreme concentration. The Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) accounts for nearly three-quarters of all mined cobalt, creating a significant single-point-of-failure risk for the global supply chain.

Key End-Use Applications

Demand is overwhelmingly driven by rechargeable batteries, making cobalt a cornerstone of the electric vehicle revolution and the portable electronics industry.

International Trade (Ores, 2023)

Trade data confirms the production story: raw cobalt ore flows from mining regions to be processed. China is the undisputed global hub, importing over 60% of ores to refine into higher-value materials.

E. Lithium: The "White Gold" of Energy

Global Production (2023)

I. Platinum: The Catalyst for Clean Air

Supply Chain & Raw Materials

Platinum is a Platinum Group Metal (PGM), often found alongside other PGMs like palladium and rhodium, and sometimes with nickel and copper deposits. Its primary source is typically highly concentrated, leading to significant supply chain risks.

Global Production (2023)

South Africa dominates global platinum production, accounting for over 70% of the world's supply. Russia is a distant second, further emphasizing the high geographic concentration.

Key End-Use Applications

Platinum's catalytic properties make it indispensable for automotive catalysts, crucial for reducing emissions. It also finds significant use in jewelry and industrial applications.

International Trade (2023)

J. Rare Earth Elements: The Magnets of Modern Tech

Overview & Top 5 Elements

Rare earth elements (REEs) are a group of 17 chemically similar metallic elements critical for high-tech applications. Their unique magnetic, phosphorescent, and catalytic properties make them indispensable. The "top 5" in terms of economic importance and demand are Neodymium, Praseodymium, Dysprosium, Terbium, and Lanthanum, primarily due to their use in powerful magnets and catalysts.

  • Neodymium (Nd): Powerful permanent magnets (EVs, wind turbines)
  • Praseodymium (Pr): Permanent magnets, pigments, lasers
  • Dysprosium (Dy): Magnets (high-temp applications), control rods (nuclear)
  • Terbium (Tb): Magnets, phosphors (lighting, displays)
  • Lanthanum (La): Catalysts (oil refining), batteries (NiMH), optics

Global Production (2023)

China holds an overwhelming majority in rare earth mining, giving it significant leverage over global supply. While other countries produce, China also dominates processing, creating a near-monopoly across the value chain.

Key End-Use Applications

REEs are critical for a vast array of modern technologies, especially those supporting green energy and high-performance electronics.

International Trade (2023)

Trade flows reflect China's central role, exporting both raw materials and processed rare earth products, while major manufacturing nations are key importers.

L. Nickel: The Stainless & Battery Metal

Supply Chain & Raw Materials

Nickel is primarily derived from two types of ore deposits: laterites (found near the surface) and sulfides (deeper underground). The extraction process varies significantly for each type, impacting production costs and environmental footprint.

Global Production (2023)

Indonesia has rapidly ascended to become the world's largest nickel producer, driven by its extensive laterite deposits, reshaping the global market.

Key End-Use Applications

Nickel's primary use is in stainless steel, but its growing importance in electric vehicle batteries is rapidly transforming its demand profile.

International Trade (2023)

Trade in nickel reflects its dual demand drivers, with major stainless steel producers and battery manufacturers being key importers.

M. Graphite: The Anode Material

Supply Chain & Raw Materials

Graphite is a crystalline form of carbon. It is mined as natural graphite or produced synthetically. Natural graphite comes in different forms (flake, amorphous, lump) and requires purification before use in high-tech applications like batteries.

Global Production (2023)

China is the overwhelming leader in natural graphite production, creating a significant concentration risk for the global battery supply chain.

Key End-Use Applications

Graphite is a critical component in the anodes of lithium-ion batteries, essential for electric vehicles and portable electronics. It also has diverse industrial uses.

International Trade (2023)

Trade flows are highly influenced by China's dominant production and processing capacity, with key battery manufacturing nations as primary importers.

N. Tungsten: The Hardest Metal

Supply Chain & Raw Materials

Tungsten is primarily extracted from minerals like wolframite and scheelite. Its high melting point and hardness make extraction and processing challenging and energy-intensive. It is considered a critical raw material due to its strategic importance and supply concentration.

Global Production (2023)

China dominates global tungsten mine production, possessing vast reserves and extensive processing capabilities, leading to significant market influence.

Key End-Use Applications

Tungsten's extreme hardness and high melting point make it essential for various industrial applications, including cutting tools, military applications, and lighting.

International Trade (2023)

Trade reflects China's dominant role as an exporter of tungsten and its products, with industrial nations importing for manufacturing.
